Traits, Hobbies & Academic Impact

1. Reading & Critical Analysis

The Hobby: Getting lost in a good book.

Academic Impact: Reading strengthens my ability to process complex information and improves my technical writing. It fosters the patience required to read through long documentation and debug intricate code, turning "reading for fun" into "reading for solutions."

2. Online Shopping & UX/UI Curiosity

The Hobby: Browsing and shopping online for the latest trends.

Academic Impact: I don’t just shop; I analyze. Every time I visit an e-commerce site, I study the layout and the checkout flow. This hobby directly inspires my front-end development projects, helping me understand what makes a website user-friendly and vibrant.

3. Movies & Visual Storytelling

The Hobby: Watching movies and cinematic masterpieces.

Academic Impact: Cinema is all about the "user experience" of the eyes. Watching films helps me relax and reset my brain after a long day of coding, while also giving me creative ideas for color palettes and visual hierarchy in my web design work.

My Hero: Michelle Obama

Background: A graduate of Princeton and Harvard Law, Michelle Obama rose from Chicago's South Side to become a global icon of leadership and education.

Why she's my hero: She proves that excellence is a process of "becoming." As a woman in Computing, her resilience inspires me to occupy technical spaces with confidence.
